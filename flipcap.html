Recharge
Flip Cap

A refillable shampoo packaging that cuts plastic waste by 80% — without cutting corners on feel.

80% Less plastic
40% Saved per 5 refills
100% Mono-material
The Problem

Premium bottles get thrown away. All of them.

Marie Stella Maris makes beautifully crafted shampoo bottles. But once they're empty, the whole thing — pump, shell, everything — goes in the bin. The brief: design a refill system that keeps the premium feel while radically reducing waste.

Early ideation — exploring refill mechanics and connection systems

Refined concepts — settling on a click-in refill cartridge with a domed outer shell

The Solution

Keep the shell. Replace only the inside.

The Recharge Flip Cap splits the bottle into two parts: a premium outer shell you keep forever, and a lightweight HDPE refill cartridge you swap out. No glue, no labels, no metal parts, no silicone — just one material, designed to be recycled as-is.

A widened base and low centre of gravity keep it stable on any bathroom shelf. The flip cap mechanism gives it a satisfying, intentional feel — because sustainable doesn't mean cheap.

Sustainability Check

Good recyclable. Lower CO₂. Proven.

Ran the design through a full circularity and environmental impact assessment. Result: Good recyclable rating, lower CO₂ emissions vs. the current scenario, and a circular business model where only the inner cup travels through the supply chain on repeat.

Recycle check, circularity score, and climate impact — all green
